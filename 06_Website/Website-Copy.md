## FAQ (order locked — biggest objection first)

**1. Is WholeClaim a public adjuster or a law firm?** No — and that's deliberate. We are claim documentation software. We never contact your insurer, never negotiate, never give legal advice, and never take a percentage of anything. You stay in control of your claim; we help you build the file.

**2. Will WholeClaim get me more money?** We don't promise outcomes — nobody honest can. What we do: help you build the organized, evidence-backed file that lets *you* make informed decisions. The founder's own documented file took his claim from a $40,000 first payment past $125,000 in covered repairs. One documented result, not a promise.

**3. Do you contact my insurance company?** Never. Every letter is a draft for your review; you send everything yourself.

**4. Who owns my data?** You do. Export everything (documents included) or delete your account, self-serve, anytime. Your claim contents never appear in our analytics.

**5. Is the AI accurate?** The AI drafts and analyzes; you verify and decide. Every analysis is grounded in a library of human-approved entries, tells you what to verify, and the prompt system is regression-tested against real, documented claim files before any release.

**6. I already have a lawyer.** Perfect — WholeClaim makes counsel cheaper by handing them an organized, exhibit-ready file. Route drafts through your attorney while litigation is active.

**7. Which states do you cover?** The documentation system works everywhere. Statute-specific knowledge starts with Georgia and grows by verified entry — the library always tells you to confirm details for your state.

**8. What if I cancel?** Cancel anytime in the billing portal; export first if you like. 14-day no-questions refund on Pro.

**9. Is my information secure?** Files are access-controlled to your account, encrypted in transit and at rest, and never sold or shared. Details in the Privacy Policy.

**10. What does the Claim Health Score mean?** The condition of your documentation — evidence, paper trail, deadlines, freshness — scored 0–100 with every point explained. It never predicts your claim's outcome or value.

## ABOUT

**The file that started it.** WholeClaim began in a flooded house, not a pitch deck. The founder — a licensed general contractor who builds from the dirt up — filed a claim like everyone else does, and got a first payment of about $40,000 for damage that would cost more than $125,000 to make right. Instead of hiring it out, he built a file: photographs before repairs, moisture readings, daily drying logs, retained failed parts, an entry for every phone call, a written confirmation for every promise. Supplement by documented supplement, the file closed the gap. Along the way he audited the carrier's own rating math and found his roof rated sixteen years older than it was — a $1,235 error nobody would have caught without checking.

That system became the Claim Binder Method, then this platform. **Everything in WholeClaim was built from documented claim records — not hypothetical examples.** Every document, every deadline, every detail.

*The results described are the founder's documented individual experience and are not a promise of any outcome.*
