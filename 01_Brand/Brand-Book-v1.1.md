## 1. Brand manifesto

Insurance companies manage claims with sophisticated software, standardized workflows, and experienced professionals. Most homeowners are left organizing photos, receipts, emails, and estimates on their own.

WholeClaim exists to close that gap. We help homeowners organize, understand, and document their insurance claims so they can confidently manage their own claim records from start to finish. We don't replace lawyers or public adjusters. We help people build better documentation.

**The purpose ladder:** Documentation creates clarity. Clarity creates confidence. Confidence helps people make informed decisions.

## 2. Mission & vision

**Mission:** Help every homeowner create a complete, organized insurance claim file.

**Vision:** Become the most trusted claim documentation platform for homeowners in the United States — and from there, the documentation layer of homeownership: inspections, home inventories, maintenance records, contractor documentation.

## 3. Brand promise

## 4. Positioning

| We are | We are NOT |
|---|---|
| Claim documentation software | An insurance company |
| AI organization platform | A public adjuster |
| Insurance education technology | A law firm |
| Evidence management platform | A claims negotiator |

The right column is a legal boundary and a brand asset. It appears in the footer, the FAQ, and anywhere a regulator or reporter might look first.

**Elevator pitch:** WholeClaim is a homeowner-focused claim documentation platform that helps users organize evidence, understand their policies, track deadlines, and prepare professional claim records using guided workflows and AI-powered analysis — built by a contractor whose own first claim went from a $40,000 initial payment to more than $125,000 through documented supplements, and who then caught the carrier's 16-year rating error for another $1,235. (A documented individual result, never presented as typical or promised.)

**Against the alternatives:** the shoebox loses claims; the public adjuster takes a percentage; the attorney is for litigation. WholeClaim is the discipline of all three applied to documentation, with the owner keeping control and 100% of the outcome.

## 5. Voice — the two-register system

The two prior drafts disagreed on exactly one thing: edge. One draft banned emotion entirely; the other led with "Build the claim file your insurance company wishes you didn't have." Both are right — in different rooms. WholeClaim speaks in two registers with one shared rulebook:

**Product register** (app, emails, support, legal-adjacent surfaces): professional, calm, organized, prepared. Never dramatic, never salesy, never emotional. This is the register of the We are NOT column.

**Feed register** (Threads, social, ads): specific, receipts-forward, one controlled degree of edge. The hook line lives here because it converts — but it earns its edge with documents, not adjectives.

**Shared translations (both registers):**

| Never say | Say instead |
|---|---|
| Beat the insurance company | Build a stronger claim file |
| Get more money / maximize your payout | Make informed decisions |
| We fight for you / we negotiate | We help you organize your documentation |
| Guaranteed / secret / hack | (nothing — these words don't exist here) |

Shared rules: numbers over adjectives; absolute dates in anything touching the record; second person; the standard disclaimer on every advice-adjacent surface; outcomes are never promised in either register.

## 6. Core values

**Truth.** Facts matter. Documentation matters. Evidence matters. And ours is contractor-grade — scope and pricing knowledge from real job sites, not scraped averages.

**Clarity.** Insurance is complicated. The software makes it understandable.

**Independence.** Users stay in control. Every AI-generated document is reviewed by the user before it's used. The knowledge library contains only what the owner approved.

**Trust.** Never exaggerate. Never promise outcomes. Always explain.

**Deadlines are sacred.** A missed suit-limitation date ends a claim regardless of the evidence. Dates get court-calendar gravity.

## 7. Target customer

**Primary:** homeowners with water, fire, wind, storm, theft, mold, or plumbing losses.
**Secondary:** rental property owners, real estate investors, small commercial property owners.

## 8. Brand story

WholeClaim was created after experiencing firsthand how difficult it is for homeowners to organize and manage complex insurance claims. The founder's first claim opened with a payment of roughly $40,000 — for covered damage that ultimately cost more than $125,000 to make right. The difference wasn't a lawyer or a public adjuster. It was the file: multiple documented supplements, each one built on photographs, invoices, moisture readings, and a written record the carrier's first estimate never had to answer to. Along the way, checking the carrier's own math surfaced a 16-year rating error worth another $1,235 in premium.

That file became a system for tracking evidence, organizing communications, understanding policies, and preparing complete claim records. Today, WholeClaim brings those workflows together in one platform.

*Guardrails: (1) The founder story names numbers and documents, never carriers or case numbers while matters are active. (2) The $40K→$125K+ result is a documented individual outcome — every public use carries "a documented individual result; WholeClaim does not promise outcomes" in copy or fine print, and the underlying records stay archived and producible. (3) The story is told in the past tense of documentation, never the future tense of promises.*

|---|---|---|

| Amber Text | **#6E5A24** | Any amber text incl. stamps (derived to pass AA) |
| Amber Soft | #F0E9D8 | Pending fills |

**Measured contrast (WCAG 2.1):** Ink on Paper 14.73:1 (AAA) · Green on Paper 8.73:1 (AAA) · Button text on Green 8.95:1 (AAA) · Muted Text on Paper 5.16:1 (AA) · Red on Paper 6.33:1 (AA) · Amber Text on Amber Soft 5.51:1 (AA) · Green on Green Soft 8.15:1 (AAA). Every text pairing in the system now meets AA minimum; core reading pairs are AAA. The original #6E7069 muted (4.44:1) and #8A6D2F amber-as-text (4.31:1) are retired from text duty — that is why the two text tokens exist.

**Typography:** Archivo 600–800 (display, uppercase headers, wordmark) · Public Sans 400–600 (UI and body) · IBM Plex Mono 400–600 (claim numbers, dates, dollars, scores, stamps — mandatory for record data). No serifs anywhere.

**Logo:** the Tab Seal — a file-folder tab holding a completed circle (the whole file, whole payment, whole home). Variants, clearspace, and don'ts per the concept sheet; final vectors commissioned after clearance.

**Imagery:** documentary realism — flat scans, redacted real documents (the redaction bar is a brand device), job-site texture. Never stock handshakes or gavel clip-art.
