# WholeClaim — Evidence Library Standard v1.0

**What this is:** the company's definition of *complete documentation*, per loss type. It is the backbone of the Claim Health Score (the Evidence 40 points), the source of every checklist in the product, and the most defensible asset we own — anyone can generate letters; almost nobody has codified what a complete claim file actually contains.

**Authorship standard:** v1.0 is written from self-perform general-contracting field practice and validated against the founder's own documented file (the $40K→$125K claim). Every future change cites either a real claim experience, a code/statute entry in the Knowledge Library, or a carrier-document pattern — never vibes.

**Format per item:** *what it is → why it matters → who produces it.* Required items drive the Health Score; Strengtheners are v1.1 bonus flags.

---

## Universal rules (every loss type)

1. **Photograph before anything is repaired, moved, or cleaned.** Post-repair photos prove workmanship, not loss.
2. **Preserve timestamps.** Original files with capture metadata, not screenshots of screenshots.
3. **Retain failed components.** The burst fitting, the torn shingle sample — physical evidence in a labeled bag beats any description of it.
4. **Confirm every call in writing** the same day. A call that isn't confirmed in writing didn't happen.
5. **Keep every carrier document** — letters, estimates, checks with stubs — filed the day received.

## Water / Plumbing

**Required**
- **Pre-repair photos and a video walkthrough** — establishes scope and condition before mitigation alters the scene; homeowner, day one.
- **Cause-of-loss report** — plumber or leak-detection report identifying the failed component, with the component photographed and retained; defeats "wear and tear" and "gradual leak" repositioning; licensed plumber.
- **Moisture map and readings** — room-by-room readings establishing water migration; defines the real drying and repair scope; mitigation contractor.
- **Daily drying logs** — equipment placement and readings until dry standard; proves mitigation was proper and complete; mitigation contractor.
- **Mitigation invoice** — itemized; the first hard number in the file; mitigation contractor.
- **Independent repair estimate** — contractor scope to compare against the carrier's; the Gap Analyzer's raw material; GC.
- **Complete correspondence file** — every letter, email, and written call confirmation; homeowner.

**Strengtheners:** thermal imaging (hidden migration), permits for repair work, flooring/finish continuity documentation (supports matching arguments), retained-component chain-of-custody note.

## Roof / Storm / Wind / Hail

**Required**
- **Slope-by-slope pre-repair photos** — including overview shots that place damage on the building; homeowner or roofer.
- **Storm-date verification** — weather report or storm data tying the date of loss to an actual event; anchors the occurrence; homeowner (public sources).
- **Roofer inspection report with marked test squares** — the industry-standard damage count per slope; roofer.
- **Roof age proof** — permit or installation invoice; defeats age-based rating and depreciation errors (this exact document caught a 16-year rating error worth $1,235); homeowner records.
- **Independent repair estimate** — GC or roofer.
- **Complete correspondence file** — homeowner.

**Strengtheners:** drone imagery, collateral-damage photos on soft metals (gutters, vents — corroborates hail), manufacturer discontinuation letter (supports full-slope or full-roof matching), code-upgrade line items (drip edge, underlayment, decking).

## Fire / Smoke

**Required**
- **Fire department incident report** — the official cause-and-origin record; fire department.
- **Photos/video of structure and contents** before disposal of anything; homeowner.
- **Contents inventory with values** — item, age, replacement cost, photo reference; the single biggest underpayment zone in fire claims; homeowner.
- **ALE receipts** — hotel, meals, mileage from day one; additional living expense is real coverage that dies without receipts; homeowner.
- **Independent repair estimate** — GC.
- **Complete correspondence file** — homeowner.

**Strengtheners:** professional smoke/soot testing, HVAC contamination assessment, pre-loss photos of rooms (holiday photos work).

## Theft

**Required:** police report (case number); inventory with ownership proof (receipts, photos, serial numbers, statements); entry/exit damage photos; correspondence file.
**Strengtheners:** credit-card statements corroborating purchases, appraisal documents for jewelry/valuables (and scheduled-property confirmation).

## Mold (usually secondary to water)

**Required:** documentation of the moisture source and its repair; timeline showing prompt reporting and action (defeats neglect exclusions — speed of response is the whole defense); professional sampling/lab results; written remediation protocol; correspondence file.
**Strengtheners:** clearance testing post-remediation, humidity logs.

## Other / General Property

**Required:** pre-repair photos; cause-of-loss documentation appropriate to the peril; contractor inspection report; independent estimate; out-of-pocket receipts; correspondence file.

## Product integration

- Each **Required** list is the Evidence Checklist for its damage category; completion percentage × 40 = the Evidence component of the Claim Health Score.
- **Strengtheners** ship in v1.1 as bonus flags (visible, not scored) so the score stays comparable across users.
- Each item's *why it matters* line becomes the tooltip — the education is in the checklist, which is the Claim Binder Method productized.
- Changes to this standard are product changes: they alter scores. Version-bump and note in the Decision Log.
